Qutub Minar

Description: Qutub Minar is a tall masonry tower in India, built of red and buff sandstone. It is the tallest building in the world made of bricks. The tower is 72.5 metres high and has five distinct storeys, each marked by a projecting balcony carried on muqarnas corbel. It was built by Muslim conquerors in the thirteenth century to commemorate their final triumph over the Rajput rulers of Delhi.

Location: Mehrauli, New Delhi
